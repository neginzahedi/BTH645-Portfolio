Questions:

1) What’s your image modification? Any thoughts (why)? Steps?
The original image I chose is one of the pictures I captured from ducks, and the format of the image is png. I decided to modify my original image with the Canva, one of my favourite image editing tools on the market. I tried to work with pixels and the size of the file. The original image has a good resolution of 1200 × 1200 px and a size of 2.7 MB. But the modified Image has less size and resolution.

2) What are the differences you notice from the modification (e.g., file size change? Quality change? etc.)
As you can see, the modified image is more pixelated than the original one. The original image has a resolution of 1200 × 1200 px and a size of 2.7 MB. After the modifications I made to the original image, the result is the resolution of 400 × 400px and size of 116 KB.

3) Is there any special consideration when you created/ drew the new image(s)? What were the steps?
Actually, I wasn't too sure about this part. I decided to choose one object in my original image and draw it with painting software. It is just a white and black duck. The only consideration is the format of the created image, which is SVG. The good thing about SVG is that Image is stretched and compressed without losing the image quality and it doesn't look blurred on devices with high pixel density.
